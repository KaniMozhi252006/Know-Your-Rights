Labour Law in India

Labour laws in India are a complex set of regulations that govern the relationship between employers and employees, aimed at ensuring fair wages, work conditions, and other worker protections. These laws are designed to provide workers with adequate rights and benefits while promoting a healthy and productive work environment. India's labour laws cover a wide range of aspects, including wages, industrial relations, employment conditions, and welfare schemes. The primary objective is to safeguard the interests of workers while maintaining a balance between industry growth and employee well-being.

The Indian Labour Law system is governed by both central and state legislations, with key legislations such as the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972, and the Minimum Wages Act, 1948, playing a crucial role in the protection of workers' rights. Over the years, various reforms have been introduced to address the evolving nature of work, including reforms on contract labor, safety at work, and social security measures for workers in the unorganized sector.

CHILD LABOUR LAW

Constitutional Provisions:
Article 24: Prohibits the employment of children below the age of 14 years in factories, mines, and other hazardous employment.
Directive Principles of State Policy: Article 39(e) and 39(f) direct the state to ensure that children are not abused and that childhood and youth are protected against exploitation and against moral and material abandonment.
Key Laws:
The Child Labor (Prohibition and Regulation) Act, 1986: Prohibits the employment of children in certain hazardous occupations and regulates conditions of work for children in non-hazardous occupations.
The Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015: Addresses the welfare of children in conflict with the law and ensures protection from exploitation, abuse, and neglect.
The Right to Education (RTE) Act, 2009: Guarantees free and compulsory education for children aged 6 to 14, helping reduce child labor.
Prohibition of Child Labor in Hazardous Work: The Child Labor (Prohibition and Regulation) Act, 1986 prohibits the employment of children under the age of 14 in hazardous occupations such as mining, factories, and railways. It aims to protect children from dangerous work environments that can adversely affect their physical and mental development. The Child Labor Act, 1986 prohibits child labor in hazardous industries and regulates non-hazardous work conditions.

Key Laws:
The Maternity Benefit Act, 1961: Provides women with paid maternity leave and protects them from losing their jobs during maternity.
The Equal Remuneration Act, 1976: Ensures equal pay for equal work for men and women.
The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition, and Redressal) Act, 2013: Protects women against sexual harassment at the workplace and provides for a grievance redressal mechanism.
The Factories Act, 1948: Provides for the welfare of women employees in factories, such as ensuring safe working conditions and prohibiting employment in hazardous conditions.
Protection Against Night Shifts: The Factories Act, 1948: Provides specific provisions to protect women workers from working in hazardous conditions, including during night shifts. It ensures that women are only employed in night shifts with adequate safety measures and consent.

ADULT LABOUR LAW

Constitutional Provisions:
Article 23: Prohibits trafficking and forced labor, ensuring that no one is exploited through forced labor, and that all labor is voluntary and compensated.
Article 43: Directs the state to ensure that all workers receive decent working conditions and fair wages, aiming for a decent standard of living for workers.
Article 41: Ensures that the state provides for unemployment benefits, old age pensions, and support for workers in distress.
Key Laws:
The Industrial Disputes Act, 1947: Regulates the relationship between employers and employees, providing for the settlement of industrial disputes, layoffs, and retrenchment.
The Minimum Wages Act, 1948: Sets the minimum wage rate to be paid to workers in various industries.
The Employees' Provident Fund and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952: Mandates that employees contribute to a provident fund for retirement, ensuring economic security.
The Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972: Provides for the payment of gratuity to employees who have completed at least five years of service with an employer.
Provision of Health and Safety Measures: The Factories Act, 1948: Requires employers to ensure safe and hygienic working conditions, proper ventilation, and safety protocols to prevent accidents and health risks.
